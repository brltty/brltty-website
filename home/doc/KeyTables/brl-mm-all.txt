Key Table: Braille Memo

The six horizontally-aligned round keys along the top are a braille keyboard.
* From left to right, they are named: Dot3, Dot2, Dot1, Dot4, Dot5, Dot6.
To the left of the Dot3 key are two vertically-aligned quarter-circle keys.
* From top to bottom, they are named: Control, Alt.
To the right of the Dot6 key are two vertically-aligned quarter-circle keys.
* From top to bottom, they are named: Select, Read.
Below the Dot1 and Dot4 keys are three horizontally-aligned round keys.
* From left to right, they are named: Dot7, Chord, Dot8.
At the bottom left is a group of four keys arranged in a cross.
* Starting with the leftmost key and moving clockwise, they are named:
* ArrowLeft, ArrowUp, ArrowRight, ArrowDown.
A driver implementation constraint requires that some of the bindings
* refer to the ArrowLeft and ArrowRight keys as PanLeft and PanRight.
Some keys are entered by pressing a set of keys simultaneously:
* Return: Dot7
* Space: Dot8
* ArrowUp: Chord + Dot1
* ArrowDown: Chord + Dot4
* ArrowLeft: Chord + Dot2
* ArrowRight: Chord + Dot5
* Backspace: Chord + Dot7
* Delete: Chord + Dot8
* Escape: Chord + ArrowUp (or: Chord + Dot7 + Dot8)
* Info: Chord + ArrowDown (or: ArrowRight + Chord + Dot7 + Dot8)
* Backward: ArrowRight + Chord + Dot1
* Forward: ArrowRight + Chord + Dot4
* ScrollLeft: ArrowRight + Chord + Dot2
* ScrollRight: ArrowRight + Chord + Dot5
* Insert: ArrowRight + Chord + Dot3
* Change: ArrowRight + Chord + Dot6
* OK: ArrowRight + Chord + Dot7
* Set: ArrowRight + Chord + Dot8
